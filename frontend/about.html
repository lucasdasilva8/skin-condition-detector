About

Born at Brown

An educational skin-condition tool — built to help people learn from a photo, not to replace a dermatologist.

SkinScan started as a project to make dermatology-oriented machine learning more understandable and accessible. Upload a close-up photo, get a ranked educational match across 22 common conditions, and read plain-language explanations with links to trusted medical sources.

It grew out of an earlier tool, Melanoma Screen, which focuses on mole and lesion risk. SkinScan widens that idea: inflammatory conditions, infections, pigment changes, and more — with an ensemble of models and uncertainty cues when the prediction is not clear.

The models are trained on publicly available dermatology image datasets and combine several neural networks so no single backbone has the last word. Results are for learning and awareness only.

Who built this

Lucas da Silva — Class of 2029 at Brown University, with interests in neuroscience and applied machine learning. SkinScan is a personal educational project, not an official Brown product or medical service.

What this is — and isn’t

Not a medical diagnosis. SkinScan does not provide clinical advice, prescribe treatment, or replace in-person care. If a finding worries you, changes, or looks high-risk, see a dermatologist.

Guidance text is adapted from established medical organizations (for example AAD, Mayo Clinic, Harvard Health). We do not recommend specific products.
